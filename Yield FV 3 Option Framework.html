Option B — Yield Spread vs Benchmark (Practitioner Approach)
Fair value spread decomposed into: ΔASW (credit/fiscal differential from regression model) + Δinflation (×1bp per bp) + Δgrowth (−50bp per 1pp, higher growth → tighter). Directly maps to how OAT-Bund, BTP-Bund, Gilt-UST spreads are analysed and traded.
Spread(i vs bench) = ΔASW_model + ΔInflation + ΔGrowth_proxy + Residual

⚡ Iran Conflict — Stagflationary Supply Shock (OECD Mar 2026): Oil price spike and energy supply disruption drive a classic stagflationary shock — higher near-term inflation + lower growth simultaneously. OECD CPI: US +4.2%, UK +4.0%, AU +4.1% vs IMF long-run of 2.2%, 2.0%, 2.5%. This compresses short-end real rates and widens term premia. Blended mode uses OECD for 2Y/5Y and IMF long-run for 10Y/30Y, consistent with Kim-Wright (2005) ACM model treatment of near-term vs structural inflation.
Option C — Fisher Decomposition: r* + π* + Term Premium
Most structurally rigorous. Three independent layers: (1) neutral real rate r* ≈ long-run GDP growth (Laubach-Williams 2003); (2) inflation expectation π* from OECD near-term or IMF structural; (3) term premium = ASW regression model (credit risk + fiscal + political). All three layers have separate empirical grounding.
Fair Value Yield = r*(GDP forecast) + π*(CPI forecast) + Term_Premium(ASW model)/100
